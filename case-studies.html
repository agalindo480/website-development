Lucero at Omni Hotels — Senior Project Case Study

This project gave me the opportunity to bring a real business to life through visuals and web design. I worked directly with Lucero at Omni Hotels, scouting the location, capturing custom photography and video, and building a fully responsive website from scratch.

From the start, my goal was to create a digital experience that matched the atmosphere and brand of Lucero — elegant, modern, and rooted in local flavor. Every photo, layout choice, and animation was designed to feel authentic to the space and the people who make it special.

I took an Agile approach to manage this project: setting clear goals each week, gathering feedback after every shoot and design milestone, and adjusting based on client input. This helped me stay flexible, meet expectations, and deliver a polished final product that felt aligned with Lucero’s brand.

Project one: just do it

For this project, I focused on learning the fundamentals of After Effects since it was my first time building an animation from scratch. I started by creating a simple Nike style logo, but after receiving feedback, I updated the design to use the official Swoosh for accuracy and stronger brand alignment. From there, I built the animation using only the essentials scale, opacity, and timing—to keep everything clean and intentional. I animated the logo to fade and grow in, followed by the text “Every Step Matters” and “Just Do It,” each appearing with smooth transitions. After refining the pacing and logo size, the final animation feels minimal, motivational, and true to Nike’s style while still showing the core AE skills I was developing.

Project two: xbox bumper

For this project, my main update wasn’t the animation itself — it was the creative brief. The critique I got was to add more visuals and make the brief stronger, so I expanded it with clearer breakdowns, storyboard frames, and visual notes to match the level of detail expected for motion graphics documentation. I kept the original animation the same, but I improved how the project was presented by adding timestamps, storyboard sketches (pages 3–4 of the PDF), and a cleaner explanation of my design choices. Updating the creative brief helped make the project feel more polished and professional, and showed the full process behind the Xbox bumper instead of just the final video.

Project 3: ninja

For this project, I kept the process simple and focused on building a clean 5-second animation that matched the warm, motivational tone I wanted. I designed the ninja in Photoshop using separate layers so I could animate the limbs more naturally. Then I brought everything into Illustrator to build the background and refine the tree shapes so they matched the vibe of the scene. Once the visuals were ready, I pulled everything into After Effects and animated the beats using scale, opacity, and position.
